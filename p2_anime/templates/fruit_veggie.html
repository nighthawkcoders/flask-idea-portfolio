Vegetables

Edible Eats

1. Cabbage


Hey we are starting off you with something easy! Cabbages take anywhere from 80 to 180 days to grow and can form six heads from one stub. The best time to plant them is in the fall (as they are a cooler vegetable) and grows best in liquid fertilizer. You have to water it 1 to 1.5 inches a day (if no rain). Once harvested you can make many yummy meals!
You can buy the plant at Home Depot for a mere $20: Home Depot Cabbage

2. Tomatoes


Next up are tomatoes. These little veggies take about 60 to 80 days to grow and the best time to plant them is at the end of August or early March. Plant deep enough that the lowest leaves hover above the ground while using fertilizer that is not Nitrogen high fertilizer. Water a lot for the first couple weeks, however transition to watering once a week about an inch or two in the morning. Add mulch to soak some of the water up to retain moisture!
You can buy them for $12 at Walmart: Walmart Tomato Plants

3. Basil


Basil is a popular herb native to southern Asia and the islands of the South Pacific and used in Italian and Mediterranean dishes and the seeds are used in Thai dishes.This little herb grows all year long and only takes 3 to 4 weeks to grow. Put the seed in a small container, filled with soil and place inside a bag. Mist every couple of days and keep the inside of the bag. When planting outside space out and mist everyday for a total of one inch per week. Place in a very sunny area since sunlight is crucial. There are multiple types of basils: sweet basil planting, thai sweet basil, purple basil, lemon basil and lime basil, green ruffles basil, spicy globe basil.
You can buy from Amazon from about $2: Amazon Basil

4. Bell Peppers


Bell peppers are delicious vegetables always ready to be part of a dish. If you are starting with a seed you need to begin inside with the seed buried an inch deep into soil. After you see it sprout move the seedling outdoors (over a span of days have it out increasingly). After 10 days you plant in soil (soil temperature should be about 65 degrees fahrenheit) them 18 to 24 inches apart. Provide lots of mulch and water (deep watering of about an inch or two a week). The outdoor sun should be good but if there are high temperatures place a covering to protect the peppers. Do not place near cabbage but great to plant near cucumbers!
You can buy the plant at Home Depot for a mere $13: Home Depot Bell Peppers

5. Cucumbers


Cucumbers are warm veggies and should be planted near cool plants. Plant the seed directly in the outdoor sun and should not be transplanted (will probably die) in warm temperature. Plant the seeds 18 to 36 inches apart and water deeply and steadily (at a minimum an inch a week). However, frequent but shallow watering will reduce overall yields. Continuously give a balanced soluble fertilizer because cucumbers are very hungry little veggies!
You can buy the plant at Amazon for $5: Amazon Cucumbers
